5. Road Less Traveled

Everything went smoothly during my application process. I was even blessed with a sizeable grant from the Federal Government that paid half of my tuition.

The next thing I knew, a new chapter of my life began. I was thrilled to learn that I had finally found my passion. I officially became a student of the Bethel School of Technology in October 2019! I chose JavaScript as my coding language. I loved every single second of school. It was very challenging and there were moments when I questioned if I was cut out to be a software developer, but I never gave up. It never felt like work. Every homework assignment and project was so exciting to me.

Not only did I learn how to build a web application from start to deployment, I also learned how to think like a web developer. I devoured every nugget of information that was put before me. Another part of my curriculum was my spiritual growth. Bethel didn't just want me to excel as a programer they wanted me to flourish spiritually. I was taught in my Kingdom Core Foundations class who I am in Christ. Once a month I had meetings with my spiritual mentor. During those meetings I began to catch a glimpse of who God created me to be. Raising up reflections of Christ's light in the tech field.

When I chose to follow Jesus 11 years ago I never imagined the roads I would travel down with Him. "Two roads diverged in a wood, and I - I took the one less traveled by, and that has made all the difference." - Robert Frost
